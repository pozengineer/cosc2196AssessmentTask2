Myers_Briggs Test

Introverted: I’m able to adapt to any situation that I am presented with. I accomplish more productive work my myself and allows me to problem solve more efficiently. If an outside factor interferes with my learning environment, it disrupts my focus on the set task. When working alone, I can undertake a set task on my terms which allows me to slow down and carefully analyse the problem and in most cases the task is successfully completed.

Observant: I focus on the problem presented in front of me instead of going off on a tangent hoping that stepping away for a bit will clear my head a solve the problem. I’m logical and practical which leads to persistence and not giving up until a solution is found. There may be issues in accepting external ideas to approach a certain task as I don’t see the big picture in the end product and the way I’m pursuing the task may complicate things in achieving the final desired result. I prefer action over discussion.

Thinking: Knowledge is the driving force when making decisions. I gather as many facts into consideration when undertaking a task which leads to the most practical and realistic way that it can be done. I believe emotions cloud my judgment when faced with a challenge. I am helpful when asked to analyse something objectively.

Judging: I like to have back-up plans and think ahead of time rather than going with the flow and if an obstacle is encountered, only then is it dealt with which could take longer. When a task is completed to the best of your ability, you move onto the next one and rarely reconsider re-doing any previous tasks. I don’t enjoy unforeseen circumstances but usually can adapt and confront it with a developed plan of attack. Duties and responsibilities towards family and work are a top priority in my view. I have a strong work ethic that places an emphasis on getting things done above everything else that may present itself.

Assertive: I would not agree with the fact that I never get stressed when faced with a challenge. Given some time to analyse and think about the problem I can usually begin to develop a path that will lead to some type of solution. There have been instances where I thought I had the situation under control and my confidence was high. Suddenly, when going over the parameters of the deliverables, my initial approach functioned but did not match what the client wanted. I felt a little disappointed in myself, but it didn’t take long for me to shake it off and deliver a product that matched the given criteria of the project.

Learning Styles Test

Reflector Style: I am more comfortable with back-end than front-end coding. This seems to resonate with learning where I collect and analyse all the information before reaching a conclusion or decision pathway. I listen and observe others before contributing my input on the subject matter.

Pragmatist Style: Brainstorming is good to get ideas on the table. You should be able to quickly narrow it down by looking at the ones that are realistic and practical. This allows actions and tasks to be assigned quickly to get the ball rolling. Once actions are in place then trial and error experimentation can take place to determine what will work.

Theorist Style: I’m logical and practical when approaching problems. Thinking rationally and objectively enables me to integrate unrelated facts into coherent ideas and implement them into the current task.

Big Five Personality Test

When working in a group/ team, I will tend to take the back seat rather than take up the leadership role. I will be very productive working and contributing to the group behind the scenes. I won’t get frustrated or stressed out with challenges or sudden changes related to the project deliverables. Initially, they might throw me but given time I can come up with a practical solution. Projects never go according to plan, and you must be able to adapt quickly if unforeseen circumstances arise. I don’t like conflict and will do my best to avoid it by being friendly and optimistic no matter how unrealistic other group members might be. I will have to be the one to bring them back down to reality. Knowing the capabilities of each group member will be important in determining the limitations of the group and what kind of project we can successfully deliver. Even though I’m logical and practical, I’m able to think outside the box and approach a problem from different angles.

When selecting a group, I will look for people who have both similar and opposite traits to me as that usually compliments each other. The social and outgoing person see themselves as leaders. This will assist the group when presenting the project to outsiders. They might become stressful and set deadlines for assigned tasks which works for me otherwise I get lazy and put things to the side. They will probably want a lot of tasks done straight away and at the same time. I will have to counteract this by organizing the tasks into a reasonable order. They might be stubborn and view certain ways in which to accomplish a task. This might not be the best way to do things when looking at the whole project. I will have to be calm and bring my facts to the table explaining logical reasons for going down certain pathways.
